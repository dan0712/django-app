Betterment Institutional overview

What is Betterment Institutional?

Betterment Institutional is a new solution for advisors and clients alike. Based on Betterment’s award-winning automated investment service, Betterment Institutional is designed to streamline the investment process and accelerate an advisor’s ability to serve their clientele. Betterment Institutional is the leading digital platform for advisors.

Betterment Institutional gives advisors the tools they need to change the experience for all parties in wealth advisory, and helps take care of front- and back-office operations. Advisor practices will become more productive, more efficient, and provide a next-generation interface for clients. With Betterment Institutional, advisors can bring cutting edge digital technology to their clients, automate their workflows, and spend time in front of customers, building new business and strengthening existing relationships.

What is Betterment?

Betterment Institutional is powered by Betterment, the largest automated investing service, managing over $1.1 billion in assets for over 50,000 customers. Betterment helps people to better manage, protect, and grow their wealth through smarter technology.

What are the benefits of Betterment Institutional?

Betterment Institutional features electronic onboarding, a fully branded client experience, web and mobile apps, automated portfolio management, tax loss harvesting, retirement income, automated transfers, automated billing, advisor dashboards, and more.

Beyond being the smartest and simplest way for your clients to invest, Betterment Institutional offers a number of unique features that will simplify your life as an advisor.

  • Advisor dashboard: Centralized dashboard to manage your book of business, view client information and performance, onboard new clients, and manage your advisor-client agreements.
  • Paperless account opening: Seamless, electronic sign up for your firm and your clients with no paperwork or wet signatures.
  • Agreement automation: Electronify your client agreement, ADV Part 2, and privacy disclosure by including digital versions in the client sign up process.
  • Branded client experience: The client experience is branded to keep your firm front and center. This includes the web application, emails, statements, and tax forms.
  • Dollar-based transactions/fractional shares: When clients transfer money, every penny is invested into the portfolio as fractional shares, putting all of your clients’ funds to work.
  • Zero transaction costs: No transactions costs and unlimited, free trading.
  • Diversified portfolio: Offer your clients a globally diversified, low-cost portfolio of ETFs where each tracks a broad, well-established index, is liquid, and has a low expense ratio. You can also express your opinions about the markets by adjusting the allocation models within our portfolio or using your own ETFs.
  • Rebalancing: Automatic rebalancing gets your clients back to their desired allocation using cash flows, or whenever a portfolio composition drifts by 5%. Because we use existing cash flows, we rarely have to make a sale to rebalance.
  • Automatic transfers: Clients can turn on automatic deposits for dollar cost averaging or automatic withdrawals for smart, tax-efficent income.
  • Tax Efficiency: When we make a sale, we sell losses (short term, then long term) first, then gains (long term, then short term) to minimize clients’ tax bills.
  • Tax-Loss Harvesting+: Daily, continuous tax loss harvesting at no additional cost.
  • Tax Impact Preview: Understand the tax impact of a allocation change or withdrawal before the transactions are completed.
  • Retirement income: Help your clients determine a safe amount to withdrawal from their investment accounts, tax efficiently.
  • Instant bank account linking: Clients can instantly link their bank accounts for fast and efficient funding of their accounts.
  • Statements: We take care of all client statements and reporting for you, including tax forms.
  • Billing: We automate the billing and will collect and distribute your fees at the end of each quarter.
  • Technical support: Technical support is available 7 days a week for you and your clients

Pricing and fees

What does Betterment Institutional cost?

There are no minimums, no upfront costs, and never any transaction costs for you or your clients. The asset management fees are divided into three components:

  1. The Betterment Institutional platform fee of 25 bps.
  2. Your advisor fee on top of the platform fee (up to 125 bps).
  3. The expenses for the underlying low-cost ETFs, which are paid out of dividends.

Betterment Institutional also provides the flexibility for you to customize your fee for individual clients.

Are there any transaction costs?

No, there are no transaction costs for you or your clients.

Are there any additional fees for any of the automated services?

There are no additional fees for you or your clients for features or services we provide or plan to provide in the future.

How are fees calculated, collected, and disbursed?

Fees are based on a client’s average balance of their entire account, not each goal. Therefore, clients can have as many goals as they would like without incurring additional fees. Fees are collected at the end of each calendar quarter based upon the average value of the account during the quarter. Once fees are collected we will then distribute your portion to your firm.

How are fees reported my clients?

Your clients see the total fee (platform fee + firm spread) in their client experience and on statements. You are responsible for providing the fee breakdown to your clients.

Are fee tiers supported?

We do not currently support fee tiers, however, advisors can customize their firm spread at the individual client level within the advisor dashboard.

Deposits, withdrawals, transfers

How do clients fund their accounts?

We currently only allow electronic transfers to and from a client’s checking account through the ACH network. Electronic ACH transfers are easy, safe, and cost-effective. We do not accept credit or debit cards as funding sources.

For transfers greater than $300,000, we can provide wire transfers. Clients can access the wire instructions within the client experience. There is no fee from Betterment for wires, but clients should check with their bank for any applicable fees they may have.

During the signup process we will create an electronic link between a client’s existing checking account and their investment account. This electronic link makes it easy to transfer money whenever, for free. Once the link is set up, clients can make deposits and withdrawals on the Transfer tab.

Do we offer checks or wire transfers for withdrawals?

We do not offer check or wire transfers for withdrawals, but we do offer wire transfers for deposits that are over our $300,000 ACH limit.

How long does it take to transfer funds?

Electronic deposits from a client’s bank to Betterment typically invest the following business day for deposits made before 11PM EST. This includes requests made on Saturday and Sunday.

Electronic withdrawals from Betterment to a client’s bank generally take 2-4 business days due to the required sale and settlement of securities. Note that withdrawal time may also be impacted if there have been recent deposits within the previous 5 business days.

Deposits must be initiated by 11PM EST to be invested by the next business day. Withdrawals must be initiated before the stock market closes at 4:00PM EST to be transacted that day. Trade requests initiated after these cutoff times, or during weekends and holidays, will be processed the next business day.

Is there a minimum deposit or balance?

There is no minimum investment and no minimum balance.

Are there limits or fees for transactions?

There are no fees for transactions. There a few limits to transactions as outlined below:

  1. Deposits must be between $10-$300,000, but clients can initiate multiple deposits. The maximum amount a client can deposit is $300,000 per business day.
  2. Clients must wait 60 days to withdraw an IRA Rollover deposit.
  3. Clients, or advisors on their behalf, can only make one allocation change per business day, as we are not licensed as a day-trading account.

Currently only one bank account, which must be a checking account, can be linked to a client’s investment account.

Do you support automatic deposits and withdrawals?

Clients, or advisors on their behalf, can easily set up automatic deposits or withdrawals on the Transfer tab of the investment account.

Do you support ACATs?

We do not currently support ACAT transfers in, but support DTC movements of the ETFs from which our portfolios are constructed. We can support ACAT transfers out and handle these on a case by case basis via our support team.

Custody, clearing, trading

Who has custody of my clients’ assets?

Betterment Securities is full service broker dealer and member of FINRA and the Securities Investment Protection Corporation (“SIPC”).

Betterment Securities executes trades on your clients’ behalf and is the custodian of your clients’ assets, and functions similarly to Fidelity, Charles Schwab, or TD Ameritrade. This means Betterment Securities is responsible for handling your clients’ funds and keeping all records of their assets, which are reconciled every day against the records of Betterment Securities’ third-party clearing broker-dealer.

Serving as a full service broker dealer provides us with the flexibility to control the entire client experience without sacrificing safety of client assets.

How safe are my clients' assets?

It’s essential to know your clients’ assets are protected in the event of a worst-case scenario such as company insolvency. If our firm was to become insolvent in the future and funds were to go missing, your clients’ securities at Betterment Securities are protected by SIPC up to $500,000 per account against those missing assets (and of that, up to $250,000 for cash).

Betterment Securities undergoes regular, rigorous examination independently both by the SEC and by FINRA to ensure that we properly maintain our customer records and satisfy our capital requirements. Our regulators scrutinize our revenues, expenses, and available capital on a monthly basis. Three separate annual audits by our independent public accountants verify the adequacy of our financial condition, the safety of our operational controls, and the safekeeping of customer assets we custody. Our examinations all check that our records match up with the independently available records from our clearing firm.

Serving as a full service broker-dealer permits us to provide a best-in-class client experience without sacrificing safety of client assets. These benefits include:

  • Paperless account opening
  • Instant bank account linking
  • Next day deposits
  • Advisor branded client experiences
  • Elegant user experience
  • Tax loss harvesting
  • TaxMin lot management
  • Tax impact preview
  • Advisor-branded statements and tax forms
  • Faster tax forms
  • Fractional shares
  • Unlimited trading
  • Zero transaction or trading costs
  • Fast development cycles

Who are your third-party clearing brokers?

Apex Clearing Corporation, formerly known as Penson Financial Services, handles over one million customer accounts for more than a dozen firms, including many in the digital advisory space. Apex is a FINRA, NYSE, and SIPC member.

How do you maintain security and privacy?

We use 256-bit SSL encryption, store all of our data on servers in secure off-site facilities, and implement systematic processes and procedures for securing and storing data. And because we value your trust, we will protect the privacy of information and will never share or sell your data or your client’s data with any third party for any purpose without your client’s permission.

How quickly are transactions executed?

Orders that are received at least 30 minutes before the market close are typically filled that same day. During trading hours Betterment will place orders with Betterment Securities typically upon receipt of transaction instructions. Betterment Securities will take action as soon as is practical following receipt of orders to execute trades.

How are orders routed?

Betterment Securities negotiate with our trading partners to execute transactions on the most favorable terms available. Its execution procedures are designed to attempt to obtain the best execution possible, although there can be no assurance that it will always be obtained. To this end, Betterment Securities takes a number of factors into consideration in determining how to execute trades, including the speed of execution, price improvement opportunities, the availability of efficient and reliable order handling systems, the level of service provided, and the cost of executing orders. Betterment Securities regularly reviews transactions to ensure that it receives good execution of its orders.

Do you integrate with other custodians?

We recognize that advisors are accustomed to working with certain custodians. However, at Betterment, we are changing the game for advisors and their clients. We would not be able to provide industry leading services and have the flexibility to constantly innovate if we did not custody client assets.

How will Tax Loss Harvesting+ affect clients’ tax returns?

Clients will receive a 1099-B corresponding to any tax loss harvests that occurred in their account during the year (as harvests involve a sale in order to realize a loss). This is in addition to non-TLH related investment sales generally (e.g. money withdrawals, allocation changes, or portfolio rebalances).

Where can I find my clients’ 5498 tax forms? When will they be available?

Your clients will receive a 5498 form if they had an IRA with a non-zero balance in the tax year that just concluded. This form reports all of the IRA contributions for the previous tax year, including rollovers. The information on the 5498 form will also be reported to the IRS.

The 5498 tax form can be found on the Activity page. Generally this form will be made available each May for the prior year, after the April 15 tax filing deadline. Clients will receive an email when the forms are published.

My clients’ tax preparation software has a limit on the number of transactions it supports. How can I use it to report all transactions?

Certain versions of tax preparation software have a limit on the number of transaction lines they support for automatic upload. If clients encounter this scenario and are unable to automatically import tax forms, the IRS allows clients to report the summary information on their electronic tax return and send a paper copy of their statement with Form 8453 to the IRS after the tax return has been accepted. For more information, see www.irs.gov/uac/About-Form-8453.

Please note that Betterment is not a tax advisor and the information provided should not be construed as tax advice, but should be used for informational purposes only. Please consult a qualified tax professional or the IRS to determine the rules that apply to your individual tax situation.

A portion of the dividend income from municipal bond ETF holdings may be exempt from state income tax, depending on a state’s tax laws. To calculate the state-specific percentages of municipal bond ETF holdings, please reference the tax information published separately by iShares and SPDR.

Please note that Betterment is not a tax advisor and the information provided should not be construed as tax advice, but should be used for informational purposes only. Please consult a qualified tax professional or the IRS to determine the rules that apply to your individual tax situation.

How does Tax Impact Preview work?

Selling shares is a taxable event—those sales could be triggered by a withdrawal or an allocation change. Tax Impact Preview shows the estimated gains and losses the sale is expected to realize, as well as an estimate of the taxes that may result, in real-time, before the transaction is carried out.

When entering a withdrawal amount on the Transfer tab or adjusting the allocation slider on the Advice tab, an “Estimate tax impact” button will appear, giving you access to a summary of the short- and long-term gains and losses expected to result from the sales.

Tax Impact Preview generates this estimate by “pre-playing” the transaction to simulate the trades that will take place across all of the ETFs that you hold.

To determine those trades, our algorithms first check whether each asset class is over or underweight by comparing your current allocation to your target allocation. The amount of bonds and stocks to sell is determined in a way that brings each asset class back to the portfolio’s target allocation.

Once the specific ETFs and amounts to sell have been determined, Betterment’s algorithms use the TaxMin accounting method to select the specific lots that will be sold. For each ETF, TaxMin selects losses first and short-term capital gains last in order to minimize the tax impacts.

Tax Impact Preview sums up all the losses and gains that are expected to be realized across the different ETFs and tax lots. If your losses outweigh your gains, Tax Impact Preview will estimate your realized losses. If your gains outweigh your losses, estimated taxes owed will be displayed.

The estimated tax is an upper bound, meaning it assumes the highest applicable tax rates of 39.6% federal, 13.3% state, 3.8% medicare surtax and deductibility of state taxes. Actual taxes are likely to be different depending on specific circumstances, including, but not limited to, the marginal tax rate applicable to you, any subsequent trading activity, the presence of other capital gains or losses for the year, and securities prices at the moment your execute the trades.

Tax Impact Preview is just an estimate, since it depends on the current prices of each ETF, which won’t necessarily be the price at the time you trade. However, it will give you a good idea of the impact of the transaction. Tax Impact Preview is not tax advice.

Who is eligible to use TLH+?

Tax Loss Harvesting+ is available to all customers with a total account balance of $50,000 or greater, including the balance of both IRA and regular taxable accounts.

At the moment, we do not recommend that TLH+ be used by customers who have spouses that also have accounts, as TLH+ doesn’t currently factor in activity in a spouse’s account when optimizing around wash sales. However, we will have this functionality in the near future.

How does TLH+ work with multiple goals?

The way we’ve built goals to interact with tax lots, you and your clients get the best of both worlds. Goals give you an intuitive way to visualize savings in different buckets, with different allocations and horizons. They also provide ability to sell the most optimal lot available in the portfolio. So when harvesting runs, we look for losses across all goals.

When clients make withdrawals, we are looking for the most tax-efficient way to sell tax lots, from the entire portfolio (regardless of which goal you are withdrawing from). Think of it as a bunch of swapping that happens under the hood: for example, “Goal A” needs to sell some VTI for a withdrawal, but “Goal B”has a more tax-efficient lot of VTI to sell. TaxMin chooses to sell the “best” share of VTI from the entire portfolio. So when clients need to withdraw from short-term goals, we’ll select the most tax-efficient lots, from the entire portfolio.

Does TLH+ work with IRAs?

Tax-advantaged retirement accounts, including Roth and Traditional IRAs, are already tax-free or tax-deferred, and it is not possible to harvest losses in these accounts to reduce taxes.

However, if you hold taxable and IRA accounts at Betterment, TLH+ does properly coordinate wash sale management across the accounts, guaranteeing that a harvested loss will never get permanently disallowed. This is a feature unique to TLH+.

Is TLH+ automatically enabled?

For new or existing customers, clients will need to enable TLH+ from the Summary tab by clicking Get Started in the overview section. Once TLH+ is enabled, harvests will occur automatically according to our algorithm.

Note that TLH+ works across all goals. It is not goal specific.

IRAs and Rollovers

How do clients transfer IRAs?

To transfer an existing IRA, we use a direct transfer process to avoid any tax consequences. A direct transfer entails a clients current provider sending us a check of the funds directly, for your client’s benefit. To get started, you or your client can click “Rollover”at the top right hand side of your Summary page. You must answer a few questions and then will receive full instructions via email to complete the IRA transfer, based on the provider’s requirements. Clients will need to sign transfer paperwork that we provide to initiate the transfer. If clients have a special retirement plan like an inherited IRA or annuity, contact us at [email protected] or call (888) 428-9482 and we can guide you or your client through the process.

How do clients transfer 401ks or similar plans?

We use the “direct rollover”method to prevent any withholding or negative tax consequences. A direct transfer entails a clients current provider sending us a check of the funds directly, for your clients benefit. To get started, you or your client can click “Rollover”at the top right hand side of your Summary page. You must answer a few questions, and receive an email with the full, personalized instructions, including how the check should be made out and where it should be sent, to complete your rollover. Please note that Traditional IRA funds must be rolled over into a Traditional IRA and Roth IRA funds must be rolled over into a Roth IRA. If you aren’t sure which type(s) of funds your clients have, contact the current provider or HR representative. You can always convert a Traditional IRA into a Roth IRA after the rollover.

Do you support Roth conversions?

Anyone, regardless of income, is eligible to convert his or her Traditional IRA into a Roth IRA. To do so, your clients can simply click on the gear symbol at the top right side of theirr Traditional IRA on the Summary page and choose “Convert IRA to Roth”. Note that clients will not see the option to do this until the Traditional IRA has been funded. The wizard will guide clients through the steps. If clients don’t yet have a Roth IRA account, this process will create one for them. If clients want to open a “Backdoor Roth”with Betterment, simply fund a Traditional IRA and convert the funds into Roth. Important things to note:

  1. Conversions must be done before December 31st of the tax year.
  2. Betterment does not withhold any taxes for you, because it reduces the amount of money that can grow tax-free in your Roth IRA.
  3. Generally, any funds you convert from Traditional to Roth that were previously deducted will be subject to income tax. Since Betterment cannot provide specific tax advice, we recommend you consult a tax advisor and the IRS Publication 590.

Please note that for clients who live in Michigan, there are special requirements for completing a conversion.

Can you undo Roth conversions?

If you or your clients change their mind prior to paying your annual taxes and decide you do not want to convert your IRA, you have the option to do a recharacterization. This is a process that allows you to redefine all or part of the Roth IRA conversion as a contribution to a Traditional IRA. The typical deadline for a recharacterization is the due date of your tax return (normally April 15 of the year following the conversion, or October 15 if you file an extension). Clients should consult with a tax professional and the IRS Publication 590 about the specifics of your situation. Clients can undo a Roth conversion by clicking on the gear symbol at the top right hand side of their Roth IRA box the Summary page and clicking “Undo IRA Conversion”. Note: Once clients recharacterize a contribution, you cannot reconvert that amount to a Roth IRA before the beginning of the tax year following the initial conversion, or before 30 days after the day you recharacterized your contribution from a Roth IRA, whichever is later.

Do you support inherited IRAs?

Yes, clients can transfer an inherited retirement plan. The process is called a trustee-to-trustee transfer. Your client’s current provider will send us a check of the funds directly, for your client’s benefit. Please note that currently, we only allow one Traditional IRA and one Roth IRA per person. For example, if a client rolls over an Inherited Traditional IRA with Betterment, the client cannot set up another Traditional IRA with us. According to IRS rules, clients also will not be able to contribute further to their Inherited IRA at Betterment.

Trust accounts

What types of trust accounts do you support?

Betterment supports both revocable and irrevocable trusts that are authorized to invest in securities. In order to open a trust account the trust has to be a U.S. domestic trust, have been previously established, and is in good legal standing.

How do you open a trust account?

In order to open a trust account, clients will first need to sign up for a personal account with you. Once the account has been set up, clients can log into their account and click the “Welcome” drop-down on the top right of the client portal.

  1. Click the “Add a Trust” button in the drop-down menu
  2. Fill in the form to add the trust account.
  3. Provide electronic copies of the title page and signature page(s) of the trust agreement for account approval.

Once clients have provided your trust documentation, our Trust team will review and approve the account. Once the account has been approved, clients will be able to link your trust’s bank account.

There are no minimums or additional costs for opening a trust account.

What documentation is required to open a trust account?

We do not require a copy of the entire trust agreement. We simply need electronic copies of the following pages:

  1. The title page which generally contains the trust’s title, TIN, date of establishment and the state of organization.
  2. The signature page(s), which contain the signatures of all authorized trustees.

Do you support multiple trust accounts?

Yes, clients can add multiple trusts to their account and access all of them from a single login.

Who can access the trust account?

The trustees that are listed on the trust agreement and signature page are the only parties that can access the account. We do not provide access to grantors or beneficiaries.

If you would like to provide access to multiple trustees, please contact us at [email protected] or 888-646-2581 and we will be happy to assist.

Please note that all individuals associated with trust accounts are required to be advised by the same advisor.

Can you help create a trust?

We do not help in the creation of a new trust. Trusts are legal entities and require the assistance of an estate attorney or financial planner to file paperwork with the proper authorities. We can open a trust account in the name of a trust that clients have already established.

Returns

How do you calculate returns?

We use a standard time-weighted return to calculate percentage returns for each goal on the Summary page, and to display returns over time on the Performance page. This return can be thought of as the amount one dollar would have changed if it was invested at the same time as a first deposit.

The time-weighted return is unaffected by deposits to and withdrawals from an account, and allows for easy assessment of clients’ investments, and a fair comparison with other investments.
