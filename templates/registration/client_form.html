    Regulatory questions

    We are required by law to ask about the following rare situations. Most users will answer No:

    • You are employed by or associated with a broker dealer.

      If you are associated with a broker dealer you will need to provide written approval from the organization's compliance officer.

    • You are a 10% shareholder, director, or policy maker of a publicly traded company.

      This information is required for regulatory purposes. If you are an affiliated person, you will need to provide confirmation that your compliance officer is aware of your account. In addition, Betterment may be required to send a duplicate copy of your statements to your compliance officer.

    • You have been notified by the IRS that you are currently subject to backup withholding.

      Most people are NOT subject to backup withholding. Backup withholding is 28% tax which is withheld from any taxable activity, such as the posting of a dividend or the sale of securities.

      IMPORTANT: If you have been notified by the IRS concerning your backup withholding status, you must state so when opening a Betterment account. Also, please be aware that Betterment cannot provide any advice concerning tax matters, all tax questions should be directed to a tax advisor or the IRS.
